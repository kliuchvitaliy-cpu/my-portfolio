Business Simulation Game with Uncertainty and Information Gap

A tool to help make better investment decisions in a business simulation game (Manahra) with an internal stock exchange within the Management class course.

Problem

Data fragmentation: Financial data and market data on competing companies were scattered across various tabs, making it impossible to get a comprehensive overview.
Inefficiency: Manual data collection was extremely time-consuming and led to manual errors in processing.
Information gap: Delayed analysis limited the ability to adequately respond to dynamic market changes.

Solution

Automated pipeline: I built a 5-step system that consolidates data from collection to final P&L visualization.
Process efficiency: Eliminating manual data entry reduced processing time by 80%.
Real-time monitoring: The system enabled tracking 16 competitors in real time with comprehensive profit and loss analysis.
AI implementation: I used prompt engineering to clean unstructured data and categorize market signals.
