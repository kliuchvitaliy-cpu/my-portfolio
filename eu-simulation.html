EU Enlargement: Fiscal Impact Simulation for Czechia

From historical balance to future scenarios: a study assessing how accession of new member states could affect Czechia’s net fiscal position, based on data from 2004–2023.

Historical Context

Stable position: Czechia has long maintained net beneficiary status in the EU budget.
Efficiency: For every 1 CZK contributed, the country received back approximately 2.21 CZK in total funding.
Impact: These transfers materially supported regional development, infrastructure, and Czechia’s convergence toward the EU average.

Methods

Data: A comprehensive synthesis of official EU sources and Eurostat data covering two decades.
My contribution: Historical analysis and a detailed breakdown of EU revenue structure (own resources, GNI-based resource, VAT-based resource, and customs duties — slides 2–6).
Key finding: Identification of the dominance of the GNI-based resource (~75%), highlighting stability as well as future challenges for EU financing reform.
